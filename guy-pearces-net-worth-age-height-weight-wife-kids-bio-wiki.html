Early Life & Biography

Guy Pearce, whose full name is Guy Edward Pearce, was born on the 5th of October 1967 in Ely, Cambridgeshire, England to parents, Stuart Pearce and Anne Cocking. He moved to Geelong, Victoria, with his family at the age of three. His father died in an aviation accident when he was just eight years of age. Coming to his education, he has attended Geelong College, where he was a member of the Geelong Society of Dramatic Arts (GSODA) Junior Players as well. Also, he had become an amateur bodybuilder by the time he was 16.

Personal Life

Guy Pearce married his childhood sweetheart, Kate Mestitz, in 1997, who is a psychologist by profession. Career

Guy Pearce’s career began in 1985 when he began acting in the popular Australian soap opera, “Neighbours.” He worked in the series for many years.  He then worked in the TV series “Home and Away” (1988) and “Snowy River: The McGregor Saga” (1993). Frank Howson who is a popular director, producer and writer cast him in the films, “Heaven Tonight,” “Hunting,” and “Flynn” and also paid for him to go to the Cannes Film Festival in 1991, to attend the premiere of his film, “Hunting.” There, the Frank Howson-funded publicity campaign brought Guy Pearce to the notice of the International Film Industry.

He then had a starring role in the 1994 comedy-drama film, “The Adventures of Priscilla, Queen of the Desert,” which was a huge hit. He soon found himself working in several popular U.S. films, including “L.A. Confidential,” “Memento,” and “The Time Machine.” He then had starring roles in the 2006 biographical film, “Factory Girl,” and the 2007 supernatural romance film, “Death Defying Acts”.

He continues to perform in the Australian films with starring roles in “The Hard Word” (2002) and “The Proposition” (2005). He performs in theatre as well, and in January of 2009, he returned to the stage after a seven-year absence and performed in the Melbourne Theatre Company’s production of “Poor Boy.” In 2010, he appeared in the historical drama film, “The King’s Speech.”

In 2013, he had a starring role in the popular superhero film, “Iron Man 3,” which was a monstrous hit. Since 2012, he has starred in the Australian TV series, “Jack Irish.” In 2015, he had a supporting role in the romantic drama film, “Holding the Man.” Apart from acting, he is also passionate about music and songwriting.  In November of 2014, he released his first album, “Broken Bones” and 4 years later, he released his second album, “The Nomad” in 2018.
